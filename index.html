01 Technical Audit

Where the stack leaks.

I always audit before I build anything.

How I audit: 60 days of routing data. Field-by-field mapping across all 7 tools. Talking to 5 SDRs and 3 AEs. Pulling API logs and finding where governor limits get hit. Then I start building.
// risks
01 Duplicate lead ingestion
ZoomInfo, Clay, and LinkedIn SN all push leads into Salesforce with no shared dedup logic. Reps get assigned the same contact three times from three sources. Once they stop trusting assignments, the whole pipeline breaks down.
02 Enrichment field conflicts
No waterfall defined. ZoomInfo and Clay both write to the same fields and last sync wins. ZoomInfo says company size is 500, Clay says 50. LeanData routes on company size. Wrong territory every time. This is not a data problem, it is an architecture problem.
03 LeanData catch-all queue
Leads that do not match routing rules fall into a generic queue with no SLA and no owner. Nobody monitors it. High-intent leads go cold in there and nobody notices until a rep is asking about a dead deal weeks later.
04 API rate limit exposure
Enrichment calls on inbound spikes hit Salesforce governor limits and fail silently. No alert, no retry, no log. Leads do not get enriched and nobody finds out until a rep is asking why a field is empty three days later.
05 Gong data stays in Gong
Pricing questions, competitor mentions, buying signals, Gong captures all of it. None of it feeds back into routing or scoring. Reps are updating CRM manually after every call while that intelligence sits there unused.
// opportunities
01 Unified enrichment waterfall
I would define field ownership once. Clay runs first, ZoomInfo verifies and fills gaps 24 hours later. Each field gets a confidence score. Routing only fires on high-confidence data. This one change alone kills most of the routing errors.
02 Gong signals into routing
Connect Gong to LeanData via API. A lead that asked about pricing on a call should jump the queue. Right now that signal disappears after the recording. Wire it in and Gong becomes a live routing input, not just a coaching tool.
03 Sub-90 second assignment via Slack
The moment LeanData assigns a lead, a Slack DM fires to the rep. Name, company, source, enriched data, direct Salesforce link. Rep knows before they finish reading the previous email. No queue-checking, no dashboard refreshing.
04 Calendly to sequence automation
Calendly wired into SF Sales Engagement. Booked meeting pauses the sequence. Cancellation resumes it with a follow-up step. Right now this is manual and happens maybe half the time. Automate it and it happens every time.
05 Catch-all queue with an actual SLA
Every unmatched lead lands in an ops queue with a 2-hour SLA and a Slack alert to the team. Someone owns it, someone reviews it. Nothing sits unreviewed.

03 Security & Compliance

Adyen is a FinTech.
Compliance is not a feature.

Every integration decision I make starts with GDPR and PCI-DSS in mind. Not a checkbox at the end of the project.

GDPR at ingestion
Every lead record carries consent origin, timestamp, and lawful basis. Leads without verifiable consent get flagged before enrichment runs. Enforced in the Clay workflow, not left to reps to manage.
PCI-DSS pathway isolation
GTM tools never store or transit payment data. Every API connection gets scoped and audited before going live. Full access trail across ZoomInfo, Clay, and Salesforce.
EU data residency
ZoomInfo, Clay, and Salesforce instances verified for EU residency in the audit phase. Not assumed. If an instance is not compliant, the integration does not go live.
API key governance
All credentials in a secrets manager, never hardcoded. Quarterly rotation enforced. Least-privilege scopes only. If a tool needs read access, it does not get write access.
Data minimization
Only enrich fields with a documented business purpose. Not everything available just because it is there. Less data stored means less exposure.
Audit logs and alerts
Every automated action logged with timestamp, trigger, and actor. Slack alert fires immediately if enrichment tries to run on an opted-out lead or a routing action bypasses normal rules.

04b Rollout plan

LeanData routing rebuild, week by week.

Routing errors kill rep trust faster than anything else. I would fix this first so everything built after it lands on solid ground.

Week 1
Baseline
Pull 60 days of routing data. Current match rate, assignment time, catch-all volume, rep-reported errors. Targets: 95% match rate, sub-90s assignment, zero unaddressed catch-all leads.
Week 2
Rules audit
Map every LeanData rule. Find conflicts, stale conditions, and gaps. Talk to the top 3 territory ops reps and ask what they manually fix every week. That is exactly where the rules are broken.
Week 3
Sandbox build
New rules built in sandbox and run against 30 days of historical leads. Zero catch-all escapes in simulation. Every unmatched case gets a rule or a manual ops step, nothing disappears.
Week 4
Parallel run
New rules run in shadow mode alongside old ones. Outputs compared daily. Every divergence reviewed. Nothing goes live until 5 clean days in a row. Rollback plan documented and tested before cutover.
Week 5
Cutover
Flip to new rules. Slack channel open for issues with a 30-minute response SLA. Daily review for 2 weeks. Rollback plan stays ready for 30 days.
What I am targeting: routing accuracy from 61% to 94%. Lead-to-first-touch from 4+ hours to under 40 minutes. Manual corrections down 87%. Reps trust the system again.
05 AI Vision

Beyond standard automation.

AI in GTM is not about replacing reps. It is about giving them better information, faster. Here is how I would actually use it, not as a demo, but in production.

01
LLM lead scoring with context
Standard scoring gives a number. I feed Gong transcripts, email engagement, and ZoomInfo data into a model via API. It produces a plain-text summary in Salesforce: "This lead mentioned a competitor three times and asked about pricing. Meeting was scheduled twice and cancelled once. High priority." A score of 87 tells a rep nothing. That sentence tells them everything.
02
AI routing for the hard cases
LeanData handles the 90% with clear rules. The other 10%, shared account ownership, new territories, acquired companies, is where routing breaks. For those edge cases, For those edge cases I use an LLM to look at account history, similar won deals, and rep capacity and suggest the best assignment with a reason. Human still decides, but with better information.
03
Gong to automatic CRM updates
After every Gong call, the AI summary triggers a Salesforce update via the Gong API. Next steps populated, stage updated, follow-up task created, pain points logged. Reps spend 20 to 30 minutes on CRM after every call right now. Automate that and it happens in seconds, every time.
04
Clay AI columns for personalization
Clay supports AI columns natively. Run each lead through a research agent that pulls recent account news, identifies the buyer context, and generates a personalization hook for the first email. Not a merged template field, an actual relevant angle based on what is happening at that company right now. Personalization at scale without adding headcount.
06 Real World Example

I have done this before.

Enrichment waterfall rebuild at a B2B SaaS company. Similar stack, same root cause.

Setup: ~800 leads per month. ZoomInfo and Clearbit both feeding into Salesforce. 40% of routing failures traced to conflicting enrichment data. LeanData routing on company size where the two sources disagreed on 42% of records.
The problem
ZoomInfo and Clearbit were both writing to the same fields with no priority logic.
Last API sync wins. No way to know which source wrote the current value.
42% mismatch rate on company size, and LeanData routing was breaking on it every day.
Reps were getting routed to the wrong territory daily and started ignoring assignments.
4 SDRs were spending 30% of their week manually fixing routing errors.
What I built
I built a field ownership matrix. One primary source per field, no exceptions.
I had Clearbit enrich on lead create and ZoomInfo fill gaps 24 hours later.
I added a confidence score to every lead record. Routing rules only fire above the threshold.
I rebuilt the catch-all queue with a 2-hour SLA and Slack alerts to ops on every new entry.
I ran the new rules in parallel for 2 weeks before cutover with a rollback plan ready the whole time.
60 days later
Routing accuracy went from 61% to 94%.
Manual corrections dropped 87%. SDRs stopped asking ops to fix their assignments.
Lead-to-first-touch went from 4.2 hours to 38 minutes.
SDR productivity went up 23% on meetings booked per week.
Ops team was fully redeployed to higher value work within 60 days of launch.
